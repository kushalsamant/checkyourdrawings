Revision checking is repetitive work: scale two PDFs, align sheets, overlay them, and scan for what moved — then repeat for every consultant return, transmittal, and reissue. A sheet slightly off alignment can hide a change you were looking for.

That friction sits on the critical path. Before markups, coordination memos, revision clouds, and RFIs, someone has to find the differences first.

---

## What this tool does

Check Your Drawings is a visual comparison tool. It auto-aligns two revision PDFs and highlights additions, removals, matching geometry, and overlapping differences.

- **Drawing A** (earlier) and **Drawing B** (later) — PDF plots or exports, up to **100 MB** each, no CAD license
- Outputs overlay **PNG** for screen review and **PDF** at sheet size for reports
- **Not AI** — no interpretation, no design advice, no automatic approval
- **Assists review** — does not replace QA sign-off, CAD confirmation, or professional judgment
- Does not guarantee every change is identified; not 3D BIM clash detection or long-term storage

## How it works

1. Upload two PDF revisions
2. Processing aligns the sheets and builds a color-coded overlay — large sheets may take several minutes; this page updates when processing finishes
3. Download **PNG** or **PDF** and review using your normal QA workflow

### Overlay colors

Blue — Additions (only in Drawing B)  
Orange — Removals (only in Drawing A)  
Green — Matching geometry (both revisions)  
Red — Misaligned overlap (review in CAD)

---

## Why alignment matters

Manual overlays fail when sheets are even slightly out of register — changes disappear into matching linework. Red on the overlay points reviewers toward areas to inspect first; it is not a pass/fail result. Confirm critical items in CAD as you normally would.

Built by an architect who spent years manually aligning revision drawings before coordination reviews.
