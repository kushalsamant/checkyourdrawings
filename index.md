## The problem

Drawing revisions contain changes that visual inspection alone cannot reliably detect.

Geometry shifts by small amounts. Lines move. Whole elements disappear into what looks like an unchanged drawing.

Discrepancies often become apparent only after review has already progressed.

---

## How teams work today

Most teams compare revisions manually—placing drawings side by side, then panning, zooming, and cross-referencing geometry section by section to identify what changed.

The process is time-consuming and scales poorly as drawing complexity increases.

---

## Why that is error-prone

Manual comparison requires both drawings to be mentally aligned before any difference can be interpreted. Small positional offsets between revisions undermine that alignment.

Geometry that has shifted by a millimeter looks identical to geometry that has not moved. Without a precise overlay, those differences are undetectable.

---

## What Check Your Drawings does

Check Your Drawings automatically aligns two revisions of the same PDF drawing and generates a color-coded comparison overlay.

The overlay classifies geometry as added, removed, or common to both revisions. It flags areas where the alignment should be verified before the overlay is interpreted.

Built by an architect to support drawing review—not replace it.

---

## The benefit

Reviewers spend less time searching for changes and more time evaluating them. Where to focus is clear before review begins.

---

---

## What it does not do

- Does not replace CAD software or existing quality assurance workflows.
- Does not read or interpret title blocks, revision schedules, or change records.
- Does not determine whether a change is acceptable or requires further action.

---

## How it works

1. Upload two PDF revisions of the same drawing.
2. The drawings are automatically aligned and the overlay is generated.
3. Download the **PNG** or **PDF** overlay and incorporate it into your existing review process.

### Overlay key

**Blue** — Geometry present only in Drawing B (added)  
**Orange** — Geometry present only in Drawing A (removed)  
**Green** — Geometry common to both revisions  
**Red** — Areas requiring alignment review before interpretation
