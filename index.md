Revision checking is repetitive work: scale two PDFs, align sheets, overlay them, and scan for what moved — then repeat for every consultant return, transmittal, and reissue. A sheet slightly off alignment can hide a change you were looking for.

That friction sits on the critical path. Before markups, coordination memos, revision clouds, and RFIs, someone has to find the differences first.

## How it works

Check Your Drawings is a visual comparison tool. Upload two PDF revisions; it aligns them and builds a color-coded overlay so changes are easier to inspect.

- **Drawing A** — earlier revision; **Drawing B** — later revision
- PDF plots or exports from AutoCAD, Revit, or any design tool — up to **100 MB** each, no CAD license
- Download overlay **PNG** (screen review) or **PDF** (sheet size for reports)
- Large sheets may take several minutes; this page updates when processing finishes

### Overlay colors

Blue — Additions (only in Drawing B)  
Orange — Removals (only in Drawing A)  
Green — Matching geometry (both revisions)  
Red — Misaligned overlap (review in CAD)

Red points reviewers toward areas to inspect first — not a pass/fail result. Confirm critical items in CAD using your normal QA workflow.

## Trust

- **Not AI** — no interpretation, no design advice, no automatic approval
- **Comparison only** — aligns drawings and highlights geometric differences
- **Assists review** — does not replace QA sign-off or CAD confirmation
- Built by an architect who spent years manually aligning revision drawings before coordination reviews

## What it does

- Auto-aligns two revision PDFs
- Highlights additions, removals, matching geometry, and overlapping differences
- Outputs PNG and PDF for coordination packages and internal review

## What it does not do

- Replace QA/QC sign-off or professional judgment
- Confirm dimensions, details, or design intent
- Guarantee every change or coordination issue is identified
- Perform 3D BIM clash detection or store drawings long-term
