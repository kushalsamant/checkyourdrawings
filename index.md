## The problem

Drawing revisions hide their changes.
A new PDF can look almost the same.
Lines shift by millimeters between issues.
Title blocks and notes get reformatted.
Differences stay invisible at a glance.
Review starts before anyone spots drift.

---

## How teams work today

Today, you align sheets and compare by eye.
You pan and zoom two PDFs side by side.
You overlay plots on screen or on paper.
You hunt for moved dimensions and walls.
Large drawing sets eat hours each cycle.
The process depends on one careful reviewer.

---

## Why that is error-prone

Sheets slightly out of register hide real differences.
A shifted grid makes matching geometry look changed.
A real edit can hide behind near-overlap.
Misalignment can hide a real difference.
Manual alignment does not scale across disciplines.
Built by an architect who aligned revisions for years.

---

## What Check Your Drawings does

Check Your Drawings aligns two revision PDFs.
Upload Drawing A and Drawing B as PDFs.
The service registers sheets automatically.
Geometry comparison runs — not AI interpretation.
You get PNG and PDF overlay outputs.
Review uses your normal QA process.

## The benefit

Changes show in a color-coded overlay.
Additions, removals, and matches read quickly.
Red highlights where alignment needs a look.
Side-by-side guesswork drops out of the loop.
The overlay supports judgment — not replaces it.
You decide what each highlight means.

## What it does

Visual comparison for two drawing PDFs.
Drawing A is earlier; Drawing B is later.

Assists review — not a substitute for sign-off.

---

## What it does not do

Does not guarantee every change is found.
Does not replace your CAD or QA workflow.
Does not read title blocks as semantic data.
Does not issue pass or fail judgments.
Does not store files beyond about 24 hours.
Does not substitute for professional review.

---

## How it works

1. Upload two PDF revisions
2. Sheets align automatically; overlay builds in minutes
3. Download **PNG** or **PDF**; review using your normal process

### Overlay colors

Blue — Additions (only in Drawing B)  
Orange — Removals (only in Drawing A)  
Green — Matching geometry (both revisions)  
Red — Misaligned overlap (inspect before deciding)
